# Yaku Archetypes

Another way of arranging yaku, and perhaps a more useful way, is by archetypes:
chi-based, pon-based, and shape-agnostic. This arrangement makes it easy
to see/decide which way your hand is going to go.

## Chi-based yaku

- [Pinfu](/learn/yaku-glossary#pinfu)
- [Iipeikou](/learn/yaku-glossary#iipeikou)
- [Ryanpeikou](/learn/yaku-glossary#ryanpeikou)
- [Ittsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#ittsu)
- [Sanshoku Dojun](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanshoku)

These yaku are all penalized for opening, and all can stack with [pinfu](/learn/yaku-glossary#pinfu) easily.
Waits tend towards ryanmen and other multi-sided waits. [Riichi](/learn/yaku-glossary#riichi) and [menzen tsumo](/learn/yaku-glossary#menzen-tsumo) are usually on the table.
These hands are the workhorses of the game; they are easy to draw into and carry
decent value, especially when closed. Note that there are zero yakuman here.

## Pon-based Yaku

- [Toitoi](/learn/yaku-glossary#toitoi)
- [Sanankou](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanankou)
- [Sanshoku doukou](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanshoku-doukou)
- [Sankantsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#sankantsu)
- [Shousangen](/learn/yaku-glossary#shousangen)
- [Honroutou](/learn/yaku-glossary#honroutou)

These yaku have no penalty for opening, they are each worth 2 han either way.
Waits tend towards shanpon and tanki waits. These hands are harder to draw, so
are usually open. They stack easily with each other and carry good value. Since
there are no penalties for opening, these hands are very flexible at first, but
annoyingly tight when iishanten. They also contribute to the following yakuman:

- [Suu Ankou](/learn/yaku-glossary#suuankou)
- [Daisangen](/learn/yaku-glossary#daisangen)
- [Shousuushii](/learn/yaku-glossary#shousuushii) / [Daisuushii](/learn/yaku-glossary#daisuushii)
- [Tsuuiisou](/learn/yaku-glossary#tsuuiisou)
- [Chinroutou](/learn/yaku-glossary#chinroutou)
- [Suukantsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#suukantsu)

## Shape-agnostic

- [Tanyao](/learn/yaku-glossary#tanyao)
- [Chanta](/learn/yaku-glossary#chanta)
- [Junchan](/learn/yaku-glossary#junchan)
- [Honitsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#honitsu)
- [Chinitsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#chinitsu)

These yaku are the ones to layer on top of the others when possible. Any and all
waits are possible, opening is penalized by one (except [tanyao](/learn/yaku-glossary#tanyao)).

## Outliers

- [Riichi](/learn/yaku-glossary#riichi)
- [Menzen Tsumo](/learn/yaku-glossary#menzen-tsumo)

Theses are your bread-and-butter yaku. They stand easily on their own and can easily
be layered on other yaku.

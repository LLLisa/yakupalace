# How I Memorized Fu

When I built my yaku memory palace, I left it open-ended; I walk up three stairs to leave the living room
and that's where I stopped. When it came time to memorize fu,
I continued where I left off: I get to the top of the stairs and there is a
hallway before me. The first room on the right is the kitchen. It has
modern stainless steel appliances, white granite countertops, and on the right
is a wall-length counter and no wall above it so you can see right into the living room.
It's kind of like a bar, but it is too high above the living room floor to be
useful as one.

Because Chiitoitsu and Pinfu have preset amounts of fu, I put them in the refrigerator. I walk over
to the fridge and open it up. Inside I see:
* Chiitoitsu: 7 pairs of pants stacked in the shape of a cube, rainbow-colored.
Worth 25 fu.
* Pinfu: A ballpoint pen with arms and legs wearing a karate gi. There are two iterations:
    * Tsumo: Its right hand is held
up, head-high (if it had a head), and in it is a sumo wrestler. 20 fu
    * Closed Ron: Its left hand is held low, below its waist (I know where its waist is because it's
wearing a black belt), and it is holding a miniature Ron Swanson from the TV show
Parks and Rec, in a tightly held fist. This is closed ron, and it is worth 30 fu.

If I have found what I wanted in the refrigerator, I am done calculating fu and I can leave
the kitchen. If not, I turn around to the long bar/counter overlooking the living room.
1. Winning Tile: On the
left, by the wall, is a bowl of fruit. There are only two fruits in it:
    * [Satsuma oranges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citrus_unshiu), they represent a tsumo
    win, add 2 fu
    * Bananas, a long, enclosed fruit representing closed ron, add 10 fu.
2. Atama/Pair: to the right of the fruit bowl is a toaster with 2 bread holes (bread holes?). This is where we
evaluate the pair in the hand. If it is a valued pair (dragon or round/seat wind tiles), add 2 fu.
3. Pon/Kan: At station 3 is a food processor, where we evaluate trips/quads. Most people actually start here, because pon/kan have the biggest impact on the final fu count. For each
of these, we start at 2 fu and then:
    * If it's closed, we double the fu
    * If it's valuable (dragons, round/seat winds, or terminals), double the fu
    * If it's a kan, we double it twice
4. At station 4 is a container of salt (which has 4 letters). This is where we evaluate
the winner's wait (because having a bad wait might make one salty).
    * Penchan (edge), Kanchan (inside/gutshot), or Tanki (single tile waiting on a pair) wait:
    add 2 fu.
5. There is a cutting board here with a $20 bill on it. Every hand gets 20 fu by default, so add 20.

You are now done calculating fu. To the right is a microwave, which represents the
number crunching. Use a scoring table or this handy [score calculator](/calculator) to
calculate the (almost) final score. To the right of the microwave are some
stale breadsticks. These represent honba, which are added on at the end. Each honba is worth
300 points (in most rulesets). Add this in and you're done.

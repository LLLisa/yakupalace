# How I Memorized Yaku

I am about to describe the memory palace I created to help me remember all of
the yaku. Memory palaces are unique to each individual, meaning that I chose
the images that had the strongest connection for me, not necessarily anyone
else. Details and help making your own memory palaces, along with a complete
list of yaku, scoring tables, and scoring guide will come in a later section.

I started by dividing yaku into groups based on how many han they score,
expressed as two numbers (open - closed). For example, Chanta is worth 1 han
open and 2 han closed, so it goes in the 1-2 group (along with Sanshoku Doujun
and Ittsu). Yaku that score the same open or closed still use the same 2-number
system (Toi-toi is 2-2, for example). Yaku that can only be closed, like
Pinfu, are grouped with a zero in the open slot, like 0-1.

## The Palace

It is recommended that you use a place you are intimately familiar with for
your memory palace(s), but since I had some experience creating them already,
I chose a completely fictional location. It's a large, modern house, mostly
white stone and dark wood, with large floor-to-ceiling windows. I am
(apparently) in the process of moving in, because the rooms are barely
decorated and there are moving boxes everywhere (I didn't actually make these
decisions, I just imagined the house and my brain filled in the gaps 🤷). It
is in the mountains and there are some remnants of snow on the ground as I
approach.

### The 0-x Group

I open the large, black door with the knob in the center and step in. There is
a hallway in front of me, with rooms on the left and right. Further down, I
can see where the hallway opens up to a sunken living room (I guess I'm in a
split-level house?). To my left, on the wall, is a group of cubbies, made of
wood, like folks might put various sets of keys in. It's shaped like this:

<img src="/cubby-squares.png" alt="A grid of wooden cubbies for sorting yaku by their open–closed han value" width="300" />

These hold all of the 0-x yaku, the ones that only count if the hand is
closed. In the first square (0-1) are:
* [Closed Tsumo](/learn/yaku-glossary#menzen-tsumo): a sumo wrestler wearing a
business suit (a _clothed_ sumo)
* [Pinfu](/learn/yaku-glossary#pinfu): A ballpoint pen with arms and legs
wearing a [karate gi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karate_gi). It's a _Pen_
doing kung _fu_, so _Pinfu_.
* [Iipeikou](/learn/yaku-glossary#iipeikou): The main character of
[this game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ico_north_american_cover.jpg)
(which I loved as a child), Ico, and he's lying down in bed. He's _eepy
Ico_, or Iipeikou.

Hopefully you are seeinghow this works by now. Most importantly, that I am
choosing images that _I_ find funny, cute, or compelling in some way. When you
make your own Memory Palace, you should choose images that appeal to you. In
the next square are the yaku that are worth 0-2:
* [Chiitoitsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#chiitoitsu): _7 pairs_ of pants stacked in
a cube. Each is a different color, in Roy G. Biv order, top to bottom. The
rainbow helps me remember that there are 7 of them.

In the third square:
* [Ryanpeikou](/learn/yaku-glossary#ryanpeikou): since it's just 2 Iipeikous,
I just imagine two of the eepy Ico characters above, lying next to each
other. One is in color and the other is greyscale, but I don't know why. My
brain just decided this and I accepted it, which is a good practice when
building Memory Palaces.

In the fourth square, which is the detached one hanging down, scoring 0-5
there are:
* [Renhou](/learn/yaku-glossary#renhou): Ren from the cartoon
[Ren & Stimpy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren_and_Stimpy_(characters)),
wearing a dress. The images in a palace can be crude if they are memorable.
* [Nagashi Mangan](/learn/yaku-glossary#nagashi-mangan): A square board, like
a chess board, except there are mahjong tiles on the board instead of chess
pieces. Reminiscent of the way a full set of 18 discards looks.

### The 1-1 Group

In the second group are all of the yaku that score 1 open or 1 closed, so 1-1.
It's an unfurnished bedroom (all of the non-yakuman rooms are) and it's to my
left as I continue down the hall. It has a picture of a knife on the door,
which is vertical and kind of looks like a number 1. As I enter the room, (I go
through each room by looking to the left as I enter, then circling around to
the right) the light switch is on the left, and it's...
* [Riichi](/learn/yaku-glossary#riichi): just a 1,000 point stick in place of
the light switch.
* [Ippatsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#ippatsu): there's a closet next to the light
switch, with the wood-slat doors closed. Inside, I can hear a gunshot go off
and see the flash under the door.
* [Rinshan](/learn/yaku-glossary#rinshan): There is a love seat against the
left wall. Sitting closest to the closet is
[Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khan_Noonien_Singh), from Star Trek.
There is a shower head on the wall above him, and he is being _rinsed_ by it.
The rinse reminds me this is Rins-han and Khan reminds me that it happens when
you call kan.
* [Chankan](/learn/yaku-glossary#chankan): or, robbing a kan. Beside Khan on
the love seat is Rob Schneider, dressed as Khan. Stupid, but memorable.
* [Haitei](/learn/yaku-glossary#haitei): A giant dwarf from
[Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_White_and_the_Seven_Dwarfs_(1937_film)),
picking up a tile.
* [Houtei](/learn/yaku-glossary#houtei): A smaller dwarf, discarding a tile.
These two are based off of a song from that movie that goes, "Hi-ho, hi-ho"
(Hi-ho = haitei-houtei)
* [Yakuhai/Fanpai](/learn/yaku-glossary#yakuhai): On the far wall is a giant
window with those hideous vertical blinds that landlords love so much.
Painted on those blinds is a folding fan, spread out to show three chun tiles.
* [Tanyao](/learn/yaku-glossary#tanyao): Against the right wall is Gene
Simmons of the band KISS. SIMmons/all SIMples, it works for me.

### The 1-2 Group

When building this palace, I completely forgot this group. I had everything in
place and I was happy with it all, and then I remembered. I didn't want to
rearrange my rooms so I decided to just tack these to the wall. So, I walk
out of the 1-1 room and I'm facing the 2-2 room (it has a pair of candles on
the door) and beside the 2-2 door, to the right, are three yaku:
* [Sanshoku Doujun](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanshoku): There is a twitch
streamer I watch called [Shoujo](https://www.twitch.tv/shoujo), so for this
yaku I imagine 3 of her, with her trademark yoshi hat, colored Red, Green,
Blue, right to left. 3-color Shoujo = 3-color straight/Sanshoku Doujun
* [Ittsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#ittsu): Ittsu can be translated as "one
breath", so there's not really an image here; I see the blank space on the
wall between the other yaku and I feel a gust of wind as I look at it.
* [Chanta](/learn/yaku-glossary#chanta): Linda Belcher from "Bob's Burgers",
who canonically loves chanting, holding a protest sign and chanting.

### The 2-2 Group

The 2-2 door has a pair of candles on the door. I walk in and on the floor,
against the left wall, is:
* [Toi-toi](/learn/yaku-glossary#toitoi): A pair of the toy spring-dog from
Toy Story. These shouldn't be left on the floor right here, someone could
trip and fall.
* [Sanshoku Doukou](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanshoku-doukou): There is a wooden
dresser against the left wall, it's completely empty, but on top of it are 9
tiles arranged in a 3x3 square, they are each a set of the 9 tile of each
suit.
* [San Ankou](/learn/yaku-glossary#sanankou): Three cans of Sanka (a
coffee-like beverage) stacked and covered by a trench coat (because the pons
in this hand must be concealed).
* [Sankantsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#sankantsu): The woman from the movie
"Total Recall" with the three boobs, but each boob is a tin can.
* [Honroutou](/learn/yaku-glossary#honroutou): There is an unsheeted twin bed
against the right wall. On it lies the dessicated corpse of someone wearing a
graduation cap & gown. This person was honored, but still terminal.
* [Shousangen](/learn/yaku-glossary#shousangen): 3 small dragon figurines on
the floor, red, white, and green.

### The 2-3 Group

I walk out of the 2-2 room, turn right down the hall, and on my left is a
door with Michael Jordan on it. There are only 2 yaku inside:
* [Honitsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#honitsu): A beehive on a stand, sliced in
half down the middle with honey pouring out. Honey = honitsu, half-flush =
cut in half
* [Junchan](/learn/yaku-glossary#junchan): I once worked at a McDonald's and
there was a guy named June (short for June Bug, a common nickname in the
southern US). This image is June holding a pink slip in one hand (because he
just got fired) and holding up one finger as he's turning to leave. One
terminal in each block = Junchan

### The 5-6 Group

As I walk out of the 2-3 room, across the hall is a door with my mother on
it, who was born in 1956.
* [Chinitsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#chinitsu): My mother was a nurse, and in
this room there is a hospital bed with my mother standing over it. On the
bed is someone who has been severely injured, such that they cannot breathe
through their mouth or nose. Instead, they have a straw protruding from the
chin to breathe through. Chin-breathing = chin-itsu.

### The Yakuman

I walk out of the 5-6 room and turn right. There are three stairs leading
down into the living room. The walls to my left and right are glass, full
width/height windows, with a door on the right one leading to a garden of
some sort. The path from the hallway leads straight across the living room,
to a set of three stairs leading up to the rest of the house, and to the
kitchen (where the fu calculation takes place). This is where I keep my
yakuman.
* [Suu Ankou](/learn/yaku-glossary#suuankou): In the corner to my left is a
swan, wearing a trench coat, covering its face with its wing. "Suu-an" sounds
like "swan", the covering means it's concealed.
* [Kokushi Musou](/learn/yaku-glossary#kokushi): 13 orphans: 13 Little Orphan
Annie dolls in a bucket, so you just see their red bushy hair on top.
* [Daisangen](/learn/yaku-glossary#daisangen): Outside the windowed wall, I
see 3 gigantic dragons, Red, White, and Green, as big as skyscrapers.
* [Shousuushii / Daisuushii](/learn/yaku-glossary#suushii): On a table with a
few moving boxes around it is a large weather vane, representing the four
winds.
* [Ryuuiisou](/learn/yaku-glossary#ryuuiisou): on that same table is a small
jade statue of a dragon with two heads.
* [Tsuuiisou](/learn/yaku-glossary#tsuuiisou): Just to the left of the stairs
leading to the rest of the house, there are 14 judges in judges' robes,
holding judges' gavels. 14 "Your honor"s.
* [Chinroutou](/learn/yaku-glossary#chinroutou): To the right of the stairs
is a cliff, with a bunch of anthropomorphic ones and nines on the edge of it.
* [Tenhou](/learn/yaku-glossary#tenhou): Literally ten garden hoes laying at
the bottom of the cliff.
* [Chiihou](/learn/yaku-glossary#chiihou): A small sumo-wrestler cartoon
figure with an absurdly enormous smile (he's "CHEEsing")
* [Chuuren Poutou](/learn/yaku-glossary#chuuren): A gate leaning against the
window/wall that has several number 9 figures in its metal work (wouldn't
that be "nines gate" instead of "nine gates"?).
* [Suukantsu](/learn/yaku-glossary#suukantsu): Four giant forks laid in a
square shape on the floor.

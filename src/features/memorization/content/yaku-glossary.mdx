# Yaku Glossary

Every standard riichi yaku, in one place. Other articles link directly to the
entries here, so each name is its own anchor — for example,
[Pinfu](#pinfu) or [Chinitsu](#chinitsu).

Yaku are grouped by value, written **open–closed**: the first number is the han
when the hand is *open* (you've called a set), the second when it stays fully
*closed*. A dash (**—**) in the open slot marks a yaku that requires a closed
hand. The **0-x** group is the closed-only yaku — worth nothing the moment you
open your hand. A few yaku are *events* rather than hand shapes — winning on
the last tile, declaring riichi, robbing a kan — so they have no illustration.
Yaku are ordered the way they appear in my memory palace. For the memory-palace
images I attach to these, see [How I Memorized Yaku](/learn/memorize-yaku).

> The illustrations show a representative shape, not the only legal hand. Where
> a yaku requires concealed tiles (like the concealed triplets below), that
> constraint is noted in the text, since the tiles alone can't show it.

## 0-x · closed hands only

<Yaku id="menzen-tsumo" name="Menzen Tsumo" jp="門前清自摸和" han="0 / 1">

Self-drawing the winning tile on a fully closed hand. An *event* (the win
method), not a particular shape.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="pinfu" name="Pinfu" jp="平和" han="0 / 1">

The "no-fu" hand: four sequences, a pair that scores no fu (not a dragon or
relevant wind), and a two-sided (ryanmen) wait.

<Tiles hand="234m 567m 234p 678s 99p" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="iipeikou" name="Iipeikou" jp="一盃口" han="0 / 1">

Two identical sequences — same numbers, same suit. Closed hands only.

<Tiles hand="234m 234m" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chiitoitsu" name="Chiitoitsu" jp="七対子" han="0 / 2 · 25 fu">

Seven distinct pairs instead of the usual four-sets-and-a-pair. Always closed,
and always scored at a flat 25 fu.

<Tiles hand="11m 55m 33p 99p 22s 77s 55z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="ryanpeikou" name="Ryanpeikou" jp="二盃口" han="0 / 3">

Two Iipeikou in one hand: two separate pairs of identical sequences. Closed only.

<Tiles hand="234m 234m 678p 678p 99s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="renhou" name="Renhou" jp="人和" han="0 / 5">

Winning on a discard before your own first draw, with no calls beforehand.
Closed only, and scored as a mangan. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="nagashi-mangan" name="Nagashi Mangan" jp="流し満貫" han="0 / 5">

A special draw outcome: at an exhaustive draw, all of your discards are terminals
or honors and none were called. Scored as a mangan. Not a hand shape.

</Yaku>

## 1-1 · one han, open or closed

<Yaku id="riichi" name="Riichi" jp="立直" han="— / 1">

Available only to a **closed** hand: declared when you are one tile from winning
(tenpai) with no called sets. Costs a 1,000-point bet and locks the hand. An
*event*, so no shape.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="ippatsu" name="Ippatsu" jp="一発" han="— / 1">

Winning within one go-around of declaring riichi, with no calls in between — so
it can only happen on a **closed** hand. An *event* riding on top of riichi,
not a shape.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="rinshan" name="Rinshan Kaihou" jp="嶺上開花" han="1 / 1">

Winning on the replacement tile drawn after declaring a kan. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chankan" name="Chankan" jp="槍槓" han="1 / 1">

"Robbing a kan": winning on the tile another player adds to an existing pon to
upgrade it to a kan. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="haitei" name="Haitei Raoyue" jp="海底摸月" han="1 / 1">

Winning by self-draw on the very last tile of the wall. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="houtei" name="Houtei Raoyui" jp="河底撈魚" han="1 / 1">

Winning by ron on the very last discard of the hand. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="yakuhai" name="Yakuhai/Fanpai" jp="役牌" han="1 / 1 (each)">

A triplet (or kan) of a value tile: any dragon, your seat wind, or the round
wind. Each qualifying triplet scores its own han.

<Tiles hand="777z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="tanyao" name="Tanyao" jp="断么九" han="1 / 1">

All simples: every tile is a 2 through 8, with no terminals (1/9) and no honors.

<Tiles hand="345m 678p 234s 567s 88m" />

</Yaku>

## 1-2 · one open, two closed

<Yaku id="sanshoku" name="Sanshoku Doujun" jp="三色同順" han="1 / 2">

The same sequence in all three suits.

<Tiles hand="234m 234p 234s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="ittsu" name="Ittsu" jp="一気通貫" han="1 / 2">

A pure straight: 1-2-3, 4-5-6, and 7-8-9 all in the same suit.

<Tiles hand="123s 456s 789s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chanta" name="Chanta" jp="混全帯么九" han="1 / 2">

Every set *and* the pair contains a terminal or an honor. Sequences must run
1-2-3 or 7-8-9; honor sets are allowed.

<Tiles hand="123m 789p 123s 555z 99m" />

</Yaku>

## 2-2 · two han, open or closed

<Yaku id="toitoi" name="Toitoi" jp="対々和" han="2 / 2">

All triplets (and/or kans) plus a pair — no sequences at all.

<Tiles hand="333m 555p 888s 111z 99m" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="sanshoku-doukou" name="Sanshoku Doukou" jp="三色同刻" han="2 / 2">

The same triplet in all three suits.

<Tiles hand="333m 333p 333s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="sanankou" name="Sanankou" jp="三暗刻" han="2 / 2">

Three *concealed* triplets (formed without calling). The fourth set and the pair
are unrestricted.

<Tiles hand="333m 555p 888s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="sankantsu" name="Sankantsu" jp="三槓子" han="2 / 2">

Three kans in one hand.

<Tiles hand="1111m 5555p 9999s" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="honroutou" name="Honroutou" jp="混老頭" han="2 / 2">

Every tile is a terminal (1/9) or an honor. Necessarily built from triplets or
seven pairs, never sequences.

<Tiles hand="111m 999m 555z 111z 99p" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="shousangen" name="Shousangen" jp="小三元" han="2 / 2">

Little three dragons: triplets of two dragons plus a pair of the third.

<Tiles hand="555z 666z 77z" />

</Yaku>

## 2-3 · two open, three closed

<Yaku id="honitsu" name="Honitsu" jp="混一色" han="2 / 3">

Half flush: a single suit combined with honor tiles, and nothing else.

<Tiles hand="123m 456m 789m 555z 11z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="junchan" name="Junchan" jp="純全帯么九" han="2 / 3">

Like Chanta, but stricter: a *terminal* in every set and the pair, with no
honors allowed.

<Tiles hand="123m 789p 123s 789s 99m" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chinitsu" name="Chinitsu" jp="清一色" han="5 / 6">

## Yakuman · limit hands

Each scores a fixed yakuman regardless of fu, and several can combine for
multiple yakuman, depending on ruleset.

<Yaku id="suuankou" name="Suuankou" jp="四暗刻" han="Yakuman">

Four *concealed* triplets plus a pair — all four triplets formed without calling.

<Tiles hand="111m 333p 555s 777z 99m" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="kokushi" name="Kokushi Musou" jp="国士無双" han="Yakuman">

Thirteen orphans: one of every terminal and honor, with a second copy of any one
of them as the pair.

<Tiles hand="19m 19p 19s 1234567z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="daisangen" name="Daisangen" jp="大三元" han="Yakuman">

Big three dragons: triplets of all three dragons.

<Tiles hand="555z 666z 777z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="suushii" name="Shousuushii / Daisuushii" jp="小四喜・大四喜" han="Yakuman">

The four-winds yakuman. **Shousuushii** (little four winds) is triplets of three
winds plus a pair of the fourth. **Daisuushii** (big four winds) is triplets of
all four winds, and is often scored as a double yakuman.

<Tiles hand="111z 222z 333z 44z" />
<Tiles hand="111z 222z 333z 444z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="ryuuiisou" name="Ryuuiisou" jp="緑一色" han="Yakuman">

<Yaku id="tsuuiisou" name="Tsuuiisou" jp="字一色" han="Yakuman">

<Tiles hand="111z 333z 555z 777z 22z" />

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chinroutou" name="Chinroutou" jp="清老頭" han="Yakuman">

All terminals: every tile is a 1 or a 9.

<Tiles hand="111m 999m 111p 999s 99p" />

<Yaku id="tenhou" name="Tenhou" jp="天和" han="Yakuman">

Heavenly hand: the dealer wins on their opening draw. An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chiihou" name="Chiihou" jp="地和" han="Yakuman">

Earthly hand: a non-dealer wins on their first draw, with no calls beforehand.
An *event*.

</Yaku>

<Yaku id="chuuren" name="Chuuren Poutou" jp="九蓮宝燈" han="Yakuman">

Four kans in one hand.

</Yaku>
